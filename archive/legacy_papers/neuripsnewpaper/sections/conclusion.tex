\section{Conclusion}

This paper began from a simple suspicion: RAG systems can improve local
retrieval scores while making answer support worse. The controlled evidence
shows a sharper result. Faithfulness is not identifiable from local retrieval
scores, not identifiable from CCS alone, not stable under a single automatic
judge, and not summarized by a one-row baseline comparison.

The contribution is therefore not a new dominant RAG policy. It is a research
finding about the structure of RAG evaluation: faithfulness claims require
controls that current reporting often omits. Context coherence remains useful,
but as a diagnostic variable inside a broader controlled evaluation protocol.
