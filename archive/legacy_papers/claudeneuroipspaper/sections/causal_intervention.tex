\section{Causal Intervention: Matched-Similarity HIGH/LOW CCS}
\label{sec:causal_intervention}

The strongest version of the refinement-paradox claim is that
context coherence \emph{causes} faithfulness changes that
per-passage similarity alone does not. We test this directly with a
matched-similarity intervention.

\paragraph{Construction.}
For each SQuAD query $Q$, we retrieve the top $N{=}20$ passages
and compute the embedding $e_Q$ and passage embeddings $e_{p_i}$.
For all $\binom{20}{3}{=}1140$ size-3 triples we compute
\(\bar s = \tfrac{1}{3}\sum_{i \in T} \cos(e_Q, e_{p_i})\)
and CCS as defined in \S\ref{sec:background}. We bin triples in
$0.02$-wide buckets along $\bar s$. From the bucket with the
largest CCS gap among triples sharing at most one passage, we pick
the triple with maximum CCS as $T_H$ and the triple with minimum
CCS as $T_L$, subject to (i) $|\bar s(T_H) - \bar s(T_L)|\le 0.02$
and (ii) $\mathrm{CCS}(T_H) - \mathrm{CCS}(T_L) \ge 0.05$.
Queries with no valid bucket are skipped and reported.

\paragraph{Pre-registered hypothesis.}
\(H_1\): \(\mathbb{E}[\phi(\mathcal{G}(Q, T_H)) - \phi(\mathcal{G}(Q, T_L))] > 0\),
under faithfulness scorer $\phi=\phi_{\mathrm{DeBERTa}}$, with
$n{=}200$ matched query pairs at seed $42$. Faithfulness is the
per-query DeBERTa entailment mean. Cohen's $d_z$ on the paired
differences is reported alongside the paired Wilcoxon signed-rank
test (alternative=\textit{greater}). The matched-similarity
construction itself is validated by a two-sided Wilcoxon test on
$\bar s(T_H) - \bar s(T_L)$ that must \emph{fail} to reject for the
construction to count as valid.

\paragraph{Construction diagnostics.}
For SQuAD: 200/200 valid pairs, mean similarity gap
$0.0064$ (max $0.0185$), mean CCS gap $0.533$, minimum CCS gap
$0.264$, maximum HIGH/LOW passage overlap $1$. The matched-similarity
construction succeeded: similarity is held at near-equipoise within
the pre-registered $\pm 0.02$ tolerance, CCS varies by an order of
magnitude.

The pre-registered $H_1$ is not supported. The mean paired
difference is small and slightly \emph{negative}; the Wilcoxon test
does not reject; Cohen's $d_z$ is essentially zero;
the bootstrap 95\,\% CI on $\overline{\Delta}$ straddles zero.
The hallucination rate (proportion of queries with $\phi < 0.5$)
is in fact \emph{higher} for HIGH-CCS triples (16.5\,\%) than for
LOW-CCS triples (9.0\,\%) --- the opposite direction predicted by a
straightforward causal interpretation of CCS.

\paragraph{Interpretation.}
At fixed mean per-passage retrieval similarity (\(\pm 0.02\)
tolerance), CCS does not predict faithfulness on this $n{=}200$
SQuAD intervention. The honest-reporting commitment of the
pre-registration applied: in this paper we do not claim CCS as a
causal mediator of faithfulness. We retain CCS as a
\emph{descriptive} property of retrieval sets and as a candidate
predictive signal, but do not assert that perturbing CCS at fixed
similarity changes faithfulness.

\paragraph{What the experiment does \emph{not} show.}
The null does not imply that coherence is irrelevant: it only
shows that the specific operationalization $\mathrm{CCS} =
\overline{\mathrm{sim}} - \sigma_{\mathrm{sim}}$ does not predict
faithfulness at fixed mean similarity. Section
\S\ref{sec:coherence_noise} provides complementary evidence that
coherence \emph{in some operationalization} carries signal beyond
similarity, even if CCS is not the right one.
