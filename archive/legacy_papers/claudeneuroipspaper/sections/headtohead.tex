\section{Pre-Registered Head-to-Head: HCPC-v2, CRAG, RAPTOR-2L, Self-RAG}
\label{sec:headtohead}

This section places HCPC-v2 alongside three published baselines on
a matched harness:
CRAG \citep{yan2024crag},
RAPTOR-2L \citep{sarthi2024raptor},
and Self-RAG via the released \texttt{selfrag/selfrag\_llama2\_7b}
checkpoint \citep{asai2024selfrag} loaded at 8-bit. Same generator
(Mistral-7B-Instruct via local Ollama, except Self-RAG which uses
its own fine-tuned Llama-2-7B), same dense embedder
(\texttt{all-MiniLM-L6-v2}), same DeBERTa-v3 NLI scorer.
$1{,}600$ pre-registered evaluations across two datasets.

\paragraph{Per-method observations.}
\textbf{HCPC-v2 does not dominate.} On SQuAD (short-answer
extractive QA, the friendliest setting for HCPC-v2), HCPC-v2 ties
RAPTOR-2L within $0.0016$ on faithfulness ($0.7084$ vs.\ $0.7100$);
both are above CRAG's $0.6982$, but the magnitude of the gap is
smaller than the cross-scorer variation reported in
\S\ref{sec:multimetric}. On HotpotQA (multi-hop), CRAG strictly
dominates: higher faithfulness, lower hallucination rate, and lowest
mean latency simultaneously.

\textbf{RAPTOR-2L pays an offline cost.} RAPTOR's
two-level summary tree requires
\(102\)--\(164\) s of offline LLM-based summarization per dataset,
versus \(\sim 8\) s for plain dense indexing. Query-time latency
is competitive, but the offline cost is a real deployment
consideration that prior single-line RAPTOR mentions in
RAG papers do not surface.

\textbf{Self-RAG underperforms in this matched harness.}
Self-RAG faithfulness is $\sim 0.57$ on both datasets, well
below the $0.63$--$0.71$ band of the Mistral-7B-based methods. Its
hallucination rate is $39$--$43\,\%$ vs.\ $10$--$13\,\%$ for the
others. Mean latency is $7\times$ higher; p99 latency is
\(13\)--\(14\times\) higher (\(\sim 45\) s tail). We attribute most
of this gap to two factors: (1) Self-RAG's fine-tuned Llama-2-7B
backbone is from an older generation than Mistral-7B-Instruct on
short-answer extractive QA, and (2) the Self-RAG decoding loop
emits reflection tokens that this evaluation harness does not
reward (the entailment scorer treats them as additional answer
text, hurting faithfulness scores). A fairer Self-RAG comparison
would require harness modifications outside the scope of a
matched-harness audit; we report the comparison as-is and
discuss its limitations in \S\ref{sec:limitations}.

\paragraph{Pareto frontier.}
On SQuAD, the Pareto frontier of (faithfulness, mean latency) is
held by CRAG (lowest latency at $1{,}021$ ms, faithfulness $0.6982$)
and RAPTOR-2L (highest faithfulness at $0.7100$, latency $1{,}296$ ms).
HCPC-v2 sits inside the frontier: dominated by RAPTOR-2L on
faithfulness and by CRAG on latency.
On HotpotQA, CRAG is alone on the frontier; all other methods are
strictly worse on at least one axis. Figure \ref{fig:pareto}
visualizes this.

\paragraph{Honest framing.}
We do not claim CRAG is universally best. CRAG's evaluator is a
cross-encoder proxy for the unreleased T5 evaluator described in
the original CRAG paper, which may favor it on these
matched-harness short-answer datasets. What we do claim is that
the popular framing ``HCPC-v2 is a strong retrieval-time
intervention compared with Self-RAG and CRAG'' is not supported
by this matched harness on these two datasets. Section
\ref{sec:related} discusses what the right comparison would look
like.
