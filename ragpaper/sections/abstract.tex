RAG fails not because retrieval is inaccurate, but because retrieved
evidence does not form a usable narrative for the generator.
We document a counter-intuitive failure mode in retrieval-augmented
generation: standard post-retrieval refinement, the default in deployed
rerank-and-compress stacks, raises per-passage similarity yet
\emph{cuts} answer faithfulness on SQuAD from $0.80$ to $0.64$ and
introduces $10\%$ hallucination where there was none. We name this the
\textbf{refinement paradox} and trace it to a property of the retrieved
\emph{set}, not of any individual passage: when refinement splinters a
broad passage into a narrow high-similarity fragment, it strips the
connective evidence the generator was relying on, and the generator
silently fills the gap with parametric memory.

We distill the explanation into the \textbf{Context Coherence Score}
(CCS), a generator-free retrieval-time diagnostic computable from
pairwise chunk embedding similarities, and a deployment policy
(HCPC-v$2$) that gates refinement on CCS. The policy recovers SQuAD
faithfulness to $0.79$ with $0\%$ hallucination while intervening on
only $16.7\%$ of queries. Across $7{,}000+$ queries spanning SQuAD,
PubMedQA, HotpotQA, NaturalQuestions, and TriviaQA, with five generator
families ($7$B Mistral $\to$ $120$B GPT-OSS), the paradox and the
recovery hold. Seven robustness checks confirm the result: the SQuAD
paradox is $0.069 \pm 0.004$ across three random seeds (signal/$\sigma=
17\times$); a one-way ANOVA over four prompt templates yields $p=0.83$;
HCPC outperforms a hierarchical RAPTOR baseline on $2/3$ datasets; at
frontier scale Llama-$3.3$-$70$B exactly reproduces the paradox
magnitude ($0.100$ vs $0.100$ at $7$B); the paradox is reranker-agnostic
across MiniLM-L-$6$/L-$12$/BAAI-bge; quantization-agnostic across
Q$4$/Q$5$/Q$8$; and amplifies from $+0.065$ to $+0.115$ as sampling
temperature rises from $0$ to $1.0$. As cross-validation, the model's
own self-reported confidence is significantly correlated with CCS
(Pearson $r{=}0.36$, $p{=}0.005$). The benchmark, code, a $\texttt{pip
install}$-able CCS metric, and a HuggingFace-loadable dataset
(\href{https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19757291}{DOI~10.5281/zenodo.19757291})
are released. The broader message: RAG evaluation has been optimizing
the wrong quantity. We have been measuring how good each passage looks
on its own, when what the generator actually needs is evidence that
fits together.
