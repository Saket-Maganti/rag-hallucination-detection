\paragraph{RAG retrieval and refinement.}
RAG combines parametric generation with retrieved evidence
\citep{lewis2020rag,karpukhin2020dpr}, and selective compression of
retrieved context \citep{xu2024recomp} reduces noise without changing
the audit question this paper poses about the resulting context set.

\paragraph{Faithfulness evaluation.}
Automatic faithfulness scoring builds on NLI-based entailment
\citep{honovich2022true,laban2022summac}, atomic-claim decomposition
\citep{min2023factscore}, RAG-specific judge rubrics \citep{es2024ragas},
and LLM-as-a-judge protocols \citep{liu2023geval}. The literature
observes that these metrics can disagree, usually across different
generations or systems; to our knowledge, this paper is the first to
operationalize the disagreement on a fixed $7{,}500$-row generation cell
with paired bootstrap intervals and a two-rater human calibration in one
protocol.

\paragraph{Retrieval-time corrective methods.}
CRAG, Self-RAG, and RAPTOR instantiate corrective retrieval, reflective
generation, and hierarchical summary retrieval
\citep{yan2024crag,asai2024selfrag,raptor2024}. Our contribution is not a
new retriever or a universal ranking; \method{} is an audit protocol so
that these and future methods can be evaluated on identified claims.
