RAG combines parametric generation with retrieved evidence
\citep{lewis2020rag,karpukhin2020dpr}; selective context compression
\citep{xu2024recomp} reduces noise without changing the audit question we
pose about the resulting context set. Automatic faithfulness scoring builds
on NLI entailment \citep{honovich2022true,laban2022summac}, atomic-claim
decomposition \citep{min2023factscore}, RAG-specific rubrics
\citep{es2024ragas}, and LLM-as-a-judge protocols \citep{liu2023geval}; the
literature observes scorer disagreement across systems, but to our knowledge
this paper is the first to operationalize the disagreement on a fixed
$7{,}500$-row generation cell with paired bootstrap intervals and a two-rater
human calibration in one protocol. Corrective retrieval, reflective
generation, and hierarchical summary retrieval
\citep{yan2024crag,asai2024selfrag,raptor2024} are method instances; our
contribution is not a new retriever or a universal ranking but \method{}, an
audit protocol so these and future methods can be evaluated on identified
claims.
