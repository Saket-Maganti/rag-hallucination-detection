\label{sec:limitations_ethics}

\paragraph{Scope.}
The main evidence concerns short-answer extractive QA on SQuAD/Mistral and
should not be used to claim the same behavior in long-form synthesis, agentic
workflows, or domain-specific retrieval without additional controls. The
matched-similarity test rejects the strong CCS interpretation in that setting
but does not exhaust all context-structure hypotheses; threshold transfer is
also uneven, so thresholded policies should report off-diagonal behavior.

\paragraph{Human calibration and judge bias.}
The human evaluation has 99 examples, two raters, and adjudicated labels: it
calibrates scorer disagreement in the audited cell, but does not definitively
validate any faithfulness metric. The RAGAS-style judge is local and
zero-dollar, but may share family-level biases with the generator; the stronger
conclusion is metric fragility, not that the judge is universally correct.

\paragraph{Self-RAG and large-model reproduction.}
Self-RAG is a harness-mismatched appendix baseline (different backbone,
quantization, and reflective decoding objective); its numbers should not be
read as a global statement about Self-RAG. The pilot's 70B and 120B
observations are not re-verified under the zero-dollar revision constraint and
are therefore not treated as central evidence.

\paragraph{Ethics.}
The audit is intended to reduce overclaims about faithful RAG systems. The
main risk is misuse: a single metric, a single threshold, or one favorable
baseline row could be cherry-picked to market a system as grounded. We
therefore emphasize per-query logs, multiple metrics, human calibration,
negative results, and cost-aware reporting.
