\subsection{Scope of the Paradox}
\label{sec:fix10_scope}

% TRACKED-CHANGES: This text is intended to replace broad claims such as
% "RAG evaluation has been optimizing the wrong quantity."

The evidence in this paper supports a scoped claim: in short-answer,
extractive QA settings, optimizing per-passage retrieval similarity can
decrease answer faithfulness when the retrieved set loses coherence. We do
not claim that this paradox governs all RAG deployments. In long-form
scientific and web-answer generation, the effect is weaker or absent, and
HCPC-v2 should be viewed as a short-answer deployment policy rather than a
universal RAG controller.

\paragraph{Broader impact update.}
The deployment recommendation is therefore conservative: monitor set-level
coherence alongside relevance, and avoid automatic refinement when the
retrieved set is already coherent. Systems serving long-form synthesis,
creative writing, or summarization should validate this policy separately.
