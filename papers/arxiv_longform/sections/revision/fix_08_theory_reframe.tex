\section{Information-Theoretic Consistency Check}
\label{sec:fix08_theory_reframe}

% TRACKED-CHANGES: Rename old "Information-Theoretic Account" section.
% Why: reviewer concern that Proposition 1/Theorem 1 overclaim causal
% prevalence and effect magnitude.

The formal analysis is a consistency check, not a proof that the paradox is
prevalent or large in arbitrary RAG systems. Proposition 1 states a
sufficient structural condition under which preserving set-level context can
help faithfulness; it does not prove that the condition occurs frequently.
Theorem 1 similarly shows that a coherence-sensitive generator can suffer
when refinement fragments context at fixed relevance; it does not establish
that all generators instantiate this mechanism.

Predictive claims are therefore empirical and are evaluated in the matched
intervention of Section~\ref{sec:causal_intervention}. If that intervention
is null, the formal section should be read only as a possible mechanism.
